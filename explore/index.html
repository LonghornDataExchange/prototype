Start Exploring

Explore the insights we have garnered on the subjects on the left. This information comes from student organizaitons and is for student organizations.

Use

We envision you using this information for several purposes.

  • Options: Figure out what other people are doing using our aggregate views.

  • Examples: Find specific events from organizations that match your priorities.

  • Trends: Get ahead of university-wide trends and changes by looking through our analysis.

  • Experts: Find organizations that have already done what you are trying to do using our events analysis.

Views

You can view information in three different forms: aggregated views, event views, or analysis.

Aggregate

Aggregate views provide information grouped by some characteristic. It will present a unique list of information, and everyone who has contriubted to making that record. It will also aggregate some characteristics (such as ratings) from the various reporters.

Aggregate views are a great place to start if you are looking for options. By looking at the aggregate view, you will see the best source of unique options which other organizations have already implemented.

Please note that some information does not have a aggregate view because the data is inherently unique or averaged before it is entered. In these cases, there will be no aggregate view present.

Events

Most the information we recieve is event focused, that is, what a particular organiztion did at a particular place in time. In this sense, this is the raw data behind whatever aggregate views we may present.

This information is best used to compare specific events or plans with another organization. For example, if you want to copy a social from another organization, you might want to see more details about the specific event that the organization reported.
